Core Framework

The Instability Sequence

ENODO identifies instability before it becomes visible — by tracking the behavioral stages that precede every crisis.

Stage 01

Grievance Formation

Detected 9–24 mo pre-mobilization

Behavioral signals in social language, local media framing, and community discourse begin clustering around economic exclusion, institutional distrust, or identity threat. These are the earliest measurable precursors — visible long before any political manifestation.

Stage 02

Identity Dynamics

Detected 12–30 mo pre-conflict

Group identity signals intensify — linguistic markers of in-group cohesion, out-group antagonism, and ideological boundary-drawing accelerate across monitored data streams. This is the transition from diffuse grievance to organized social force.

Stage 03

Legitimacy Erosion

Detected 6–18 mo pre-breakdown

Institutional trust signals deteriorate across government, media, financial, and civil structures simultaneously. ENODO tracks legitimacy erosion as a behavioral composite — the stage at which grievance and identity have fused into systemic delegitimization.

Stage 04

Conflict / Crisis

Detected: too late for most institutional actors

Observable conflict, political rupture, or market dislocation becomes measurable by conventional risk models — typically 6 to 30 months after ENODO first detected the sequence. By Stage 4, repositioning windows have closed for most institutional actors.

Conventional risk models register Stage 4. ENODO detects Stage 1.
